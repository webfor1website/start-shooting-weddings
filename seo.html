SEO for Wedding Photographers

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is how couples find you on Google. This guide walks you through registering your website with Google, optimizing your pages, and ranking for local wedding photography searches in your area.

Google Search Console

Google Search Console is a free tool that lets you monitor how Google sees your website. It's essential for SEO.

Step 1 — Create a Google Account

If you don't already have one, create a Google account at accounts.google.com. Use a professional email address — yourname@yourdomain.com is best.

Step 2 — Go to Google Search Console

Visit search.google.com/search-console and click "Start now". Sign in with your Google account.

Step 3 — Add your website

Enter your website URL. Use the full URL with https:// — for example: https://yourweddingphotography.com

Step 4 — Verify ownership

Google needs to verify you own the website. The easiest method is "HTML file upload":

1. Download the HTML verification file from Google.
2. Upload it to your website's root directory (the same folder as index.html).
3. Click "Verify" in Search Console.

Step 5 — Submit your sitemap

A sitemap tells Google about all the pages on your website. After verification:

1. In Search Console, go to "Sitemaps" in the left menu.
2. Enter your sitemap URL: sitemap.xml
3. Click "Submit".

If you don't have a sitemap.xml file, you can create one using free online sitemap generators or skip this step for now.

Step 6 — Request indexing

After adding your website, request that Google index your homepage:

1. In Search Console, go to "URL Inspection".
2. Enter your homepage URL.
3. Click "Request Indexing".

This tells Google to crawl and add your site to their search results.

On-Page SEO Basics

Each page on your website needs proper SEO elements to rank well.

Title Tags

The title tag appears in browser tabs and Google search results. It should be 50-60 characters and include your main keyword.

Good example: "Phoenix Wedding Photographer | Your Name"

Bad example: "Home"

Meta Descriptions

The meta description appears below your title in search results. It should be 150-160 characters and describe what the page offers.

Good example: "Professional wedding photographer in Phoenix, Arizona. Capturing authentic moments and creating timeless memories for couples across the Valley."

Header Tags (H1, H2, H3)

Use header tags to structure your content. Each page should have one H1 (main heading), followed by H2s for sections, and H3s for subsections.

Include your location and service keywords naturally in headers.

Image Alt Text

Alt text describes images for search engines and screen readers. Every image on your site should have descriptive alt text.

Good example: "Bride and groom holding hands during golden hour sunset wedding in Phoenix, Arizona"

Bad example: "wedding-001.jpg"

URL Structure

Keep URLs short, descriptive, and include keywords when possible.

Good example: yoursite.com/phoenix-wedding-photography

Bad example: yoursite.com/page?id=123

Mobile-Friendly Design

Google prioritizes mobile-friendly websites. Your site must work perfectly on phones and tablets. Test it at search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly

Local SEO for Wedding Photographers

Most couples search for wedding photographers in their specific location. Local SEO helps you rank for these searches.

Google Business Profile

Create a free Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business). This is essential for local SEO.

1. Go to business.google.com
2. Claim or create your business listing
3. Add your address, phone, hours, and website
4. Upload photos of your work
5. Collect reviews from past clients

NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone. Your business information must be identical everywhere online:

• Your website
• Google Business Profile
• Social media profiles
• Wedding directories (The Knot, WeddingWire, etc.)

Location-Specific Pages

Create pages for each area you serve. If you cover multiple cities, create a page for each:

• Phoenix Wedding Photography
• Scottsdale Wedding Photography
• Tempe Wedding Photography
• Gilbert Wedding Photography

Each page should have unique content about that location.

Wedding Directory Listings

List your business on wedding directories. These provide backlinks and help with local visibility:

• The Knot
• WeddingWire
• Zola
• Here Comes The Guide
• WeddingSpot

Local Keywords

Include your location naturally throughout your website:

• "Wedding photographer in [City]"
• "[City] wedding photography"
• "Serving [City], [City], and surrounding areas"

Get Reviews

Google reviews are a major ranking factor for local SEO. After each wedding, ask your couple to leave a review on your Google Business Profile.

Send them a direct link to make it easy. Aim for at least 10-20 reviews to start building authority.

Image Optimization

As a photographer, your images are your most important asset. Optimize them for both users and search engines.

File Size

Large image files slow down your website. Google penalizes slow sites. Compress images before uploading:

• Use tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim
• Aim for under 200KB per image
• Use WebP format when possible

File Names

Name your images descriptively before uploading:

Good: phoenix-wedding-photography-bride-groom.jpg

Bad: IMG_4521.jpg

Use hyphens, not underscores or spaces.

Alt Text

Write descriptive alt text for every image. Include your location and relevant keywords naturally.

Describe what's happening in the image, not just listing keywords.

Lazy Loading

Implement lazy loading so images load as the user scrolls down the page. This improves initial page load speed.

Many website builders and plugins offer this feature automatically.

Content Strategy

Creating valuable content helps you rank for more keywords and establishes your expertise.

Blog Posts

Write blog posts about topics couples search for:

• "Best wedding venues in [City]"
• "Wedding photography timeline for [City] weddings"
• "What to look for in a wedding photographer"
• "Wedding photography packages explained"

Real Wedding Features

Feature real weddings you've photographed. Each feature should include:

• Couple names and wedding date
• Venue name and location
• Vendor list (florist, planner, etc.)
• Story of the day
• Gallery of 20-30 images

Tag the venue and vendors — they may share your post, giving you exposure.

FAQ Pages

Create an FAQ page answering common questions couples ask:

• How many weddings do you photograph per year?
• What is your photography style?
• Do you have backup equipment?
• How long does it take to receive photos?

Location Guides

Create guides for popular wedding venues in your area:

• "Photography tips for [Venue Name]"
• "Best photo spots at [Venue Name]"
• "Timeline for weddings at [Venue Name]"

Vendors and couples searching for these venues will find your content.

Technical SEO Basics

Technical SEO ensures search engines can crawl and understand your website.

Page Speed

Fast-loading pages rank higher. Test your speed at pagespeed.web.dev

• Aim for a score above 90 on both mobile and desktop
• Compress images
• Minimize CSS and JavaScript
• Use a fast web host

SSL Certificate

Your website must use HTTPS (not HTTP). This is a ranking factor and builds trust with visitors.

Most web hosts provide free SSL certificates via Let's Encrypt. If your site doesn't have HTTPS, contact your host.

Mobile Responsiveness

Your website must work perfectly on mobile devices. Google uses mobile-first indexing.

Test your site at search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly

404 Errors

Broken links (404 errors) hurt user experience and SEO. Check Google Search Console for 404 errors and fix them:

• Redirect broken URLs to relevant pages
• Update internal links
• Remove broken external links

Sitemap

A sitemap.xml file lists all pages on your website. Submit it to Google Search Console to help Google discover your content.

Use a sitemap generator tool if your website builder doesn't create one automatically.

Robots.txt

A robots.txt file tells search engines which pages they can and cannot crawl. Most websites don't need to modify this, but ensure it's not blocking important pages.

Check yoursite.com/robots.txt to see yours.

Measuring Success

Track your SEO progress to see what's working and what needs improvement.

Google Search Console

Monitor these metrics in Search Console:

• Performance: Clicks, impressions, average position
• Coverage: Which pages are indexed
• Mobile Usability: Mobile issues
• Enhancements: Structured data and AMP

Google Analytics

Set up Google Analytics to track website traffic:

• Number of visitors
• Where traffic comes from (Google, social, direct)
• Which pages are most popular
• How long visitors stay on your site

Rank Tracking

Track your rankings for important keywords:

• "[City] wedding photographer"
• "Wedding photography [City]"
• Your business name

Use free tools like Google Search Console or paid tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs.

Realistic Timeline

SEO takes time. Don't expect overnight results:

• 1-3 months: Google indexes your site, you appear for your business name
• 3-6 months: You start ranking for long-tail keywords
• 6-12 months: You rank for competitive local keywords
• 12+ months: Strong local rankings and consistent organic traffic
