On Camera and Off Camera Flash for Wedding Photography

Flash Sync Speed

Most important rule in this section.

Never exceed 1/180 shutter speed when using flash.

The mechanical shutter has two curtains. Above 1/180 the second curtain starts closing before the first finishes opening. The flash fires but only part of the sensor is exposed. You get a dark band across the bottom of the image. Unrecoverable.

Canon manual says 1/200. Real world experience across 150 weddings says 1/180.

Use 1/180 as your hard ceiling. Lock it. Never move it when using flash.

ETTL vs Manual Flash

ETTL

Camera and flash communicate automatically. Camera meters, fires preflash, calculates correct exposure, fires main flash.

Good for candids and getting ready.

Can be fooled by very bright or dark backgrounds.

Set on 580EX II:

MODE button until LCD shows E-TTL II

Manual Flash

You set power yourself.

Full power = 1/1

Half power = 1/2

Quarter power = 1/4

Down to 1/128

Consistent exposure all night.

Right choice for off camera reception lighting.

Set on 580EX II:

MODE button until LCD shows M

Use dial to adjust power level

Flash Exposure Compensation

In ETTL dial exposure up or down using SEL/SET dial.

Plus = brighter

Minus = darker

On Camera Flash

Speedlight sits in hot shoe on top of camera.

Direct flash = harsh flat light.

Bounced flash = soft natural light.

To bounce:

Tilt flash head toward ceiling or nearest wall.

Light bounces and spreads.

Much more flattering than direct flash.

Off Camera Reception Lighting System

Equipment needed:

4x Canon Speedlight 580EX II

5x Yongnuo RF603C II triggers

4x Impact 6ft light stands

4x hot shoe speedlite brackets

Setup:

One trigger on camera hot shoe.

One trigger on each speedlight.

Camera trigger fires all four simultaneously.

Place stands in corners or around perimeter of reception space.

Best time to setup lighting:

Setup reception lighting during cocktail hour — approximately 45 minutes before reception begins.

This gives you time to place stands, test triggers, dial in power levels, and adjust for the room before guests enter.

Never wait until guests are in the room. Setting up stands around dancing guests is awkward and unprofessional.

If cocktail hour is in the same space as reception, setup during the ceremony instead.

Keep your lighting bag packed between weddings. Everything should be ready to deploy immediately.

Reception Settings

Lock these first and do not move them:

Lens: 50mm

Aperture: f/2.5 to f/2.8

Shutter: 1/180 — never move this

ISO: start at 320-800

Then work the flashes:

Start all four at 1/16 power

Too dark — bump to 1/8

Still too dark — boost ISO one third of a stop at a time

Try to stay between ISO 320 and 800

Work the flashes first. ISO is your last adjustment when using off camera flash.

What is a Stop

A stop is a doubling or halving of light.

One stop brighter = twice as much light.

One stop darker = half as much light.

A third of a stop:

Each full stop divided into thirds for fine tuning.

Between ISO 800 and ISO 1600:

ISO 1000 = one third stop up

ISO 1250 = two thirds stop up

ISO 1600 = full stop up

Canon dials move in third stop increments automatically.

Why This System Works

Lock your camera.

Work your flashes.

Once dialed in for the room your exposure stays consistent from any angle all night long.

First dance, toasts, dancing — same settings, same results.
