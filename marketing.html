Marketing Your Wedding Photography Business

Facebook and Instagram Boosted Posts

Boosting a post means paying Facebook or Instagram to show your post to people beyond your current followers.

How to Boost on Instagram

Post a strong portfolio image. Tap Boost Post below it. Choose your goal: Select More Profile Visits or More Website Visits. Define your audience: Location: your city and surrounding 50 miles. Age: 22 to 35. Interests: weddings, engaged, recently engaged. Set your budget: Start with $5 to $10 per day. Run for 7 days. Total spend: $35 to $70. Monitor results in Instagram Insights.

How to Boost on Facebook

Go to your Facebook business page. Post a portfolio image or client testimonial. Click Boost Post. Same audience targeting as Instagram. Facebook and Instagram share the same ad platform — you can run both simultaneously from Facebook Ads Manager.

What to Boost

Your best portfolio images. Real wedding features. Client testimonials. Before and after edits. Behind the scenes.

What Not to Boost

Pricing posts — let them come to you first. Generic stock looking images. Anything with text overlaid on the image — Facebook reduces reach on text heavy images.

Budget Recommendation for Beginners

$50 per month total. Split across three to four posts. $10 to $15 per boost. Run each for 7 days. Track which posts get the most profile visits and website clicks. Boost more of what works.

Google Ads

Google ads appear at the top of search results when couples search for photographers.

How to Set Up

Go to ads.google.com. Create an account. Click New Campaign. Choose Search campaign. Goal: Website traffic.

Keywords to Target

wedding photographer [your city]. wedding photography [your city]. affordable wedding photographer [your city].

Budget

Start at $5 per day. $150 per month. Google charges per click not per impression. Average cost per click for wedding photography: $2 to $5.

Ad Copy That Works

Headline 1: Scottsdale Wedding Photographer
Headline 2: Packages From $1,250
Headline 3: 150 Weddings. Book Your Date.
Description: Bright airy documentary style. Real moments. No stiff poses. Free consultation. Limited dates available.

The Knot and WeddingWire Advertising

Both platforms offer paid advertising tiers above the free listings.

Pricing Tiers

Free listing — always do this first. Paid storefront — $100 to $300 per month depending on market. Featured placement — appears at top of search results.

When to Upgrade to Paid

Not until you have at least five real reviews on the platform. Reviews drive conversions more than paid placement. Get reviews first. Upgrade later.

How to Get Reviews

After every wedding send a personal email to the couple: "It was such an honor to photograph your wedding. If you have a moment I would really appreciate a review on The Knot — it helps other couples find me." Include a direct link to your review page. Most happy couples will leave one.

What Actually Works

In order of ROI for wedding photographers:

1. Word of Mouth

Free, highest conversion rate. Ask every couple to refer you to friends.

2. Instagram

Free, high discovery rate. Post consistently. Tag venues. Use location hashtags.

3. The Knot and WeddingWire Free Listings

Free, high intent traffic.

4. Google Search

Organic SEO, free but slow. Takes 6 to 12 months to see results.

5. Boosted Instagram Posts

$50 per month, good for brand awareness.

6. Google Ads

$150 per month, good for direct bookings.

7. The Knot Paid Placement

$200 per month, only worth it with reviews.

Start with 1 through 4. They are all free. Add paid advertising only after you have five or more reviews and a portfolio of at least three paid weddings.
