On The Day Guide — Posing, Shot Lists, and Working With Groups

This page covers everything you need to walk into a wedding day confident and prepared. Poses that work on anyone. A shot list so nothing gets missed. A family portrait system that keeps things moving. And bridal party recommendations that actually look good.

Posing

The goal of posing is never a stiff catalog image. The goal is finding the natural version of a moment and guiding people toward it.

Give Direction Not Positions

Instead of "put your hand here" say "walk toward me slowly" or "whisper something in her ear." Movement creates natural moments. Natural moments create portfolio images.

The Five Poses That Work on Every Couple

POSE 1 — The Walk

Direction: "Just walk toward me slowly together. Look at each other, not at me."

Works everywhere. Creates movement and natural interaction. Shoot at f/2.8 on 85mm. Fire continuously as they walk.

POSE 2 — The Forehead Touch

Direction: "Face each other. Close your eyes. Touch foreheads gently."

Incredibly intimate. Works in any location. Shoot tight on 85mm at f/2.2. Wait for a natural smile or laugh to emerge.

POSE 3 — The Look Away

Direction: "Stand together. Both of you look out at the view. Forget I'm here."

Creates a cinematic editorial feel. Works best with a strong background — venue, landscape, architecture.

POSE 4 — The Candid Laugh

Direction: Tell them a terrible joke. Or ask them to tell each other an inside joke. Or say "on three, both of you make the most ridiculous face possible."

The laugh that follows is always real. Real laughs photograph beautifully.

POSE 5 — The Quiet Moment

Direction: "Just hold each other. Don't pose. Just be here for thirty seconds."

Step back. Shoot from a distance with 85mm. These images are often the favorites from the day.

General Posing Tips

Body Position

Slight angle to camera — never fully straight on. Weight on back foot — creates relaxed natural stance. Space between arms and body — arms flat against sides looks stiff.

Face and Hands

Chin slightly forward and down — eliminates double chin, works on everyone. Hands need to be doing something — holding, touching, interacting.

Eyes Matter Most

Eyes matter more than anything else in a portrait. Always check where the eyes are before you shoot.

Family Portrait Shot List

Family formals are the most stressful part of the wedding day. They don't have to be.

Before the Wedding

Email the couple a simple form. Ask them to list everyone who needs to be in a formal photo. Ask them to organize by grouping. Tell them family formals will take 20 to 30 minutes if everyone is ready and present.

Strategy

Start with the largest group and work down to smallest. Dismiss people as their groupings are complete so they can enjoy cocktail hour.

Tips for Efficient Family Formals

Assign a Family Wrangler

Someone who knows all the family members and can gather groups. Not you. A bridesmaid or family member who is loud and organized.

Keep Moving

Call groups loudly and clearly. Keep energy positive and moving. "Okay that's perfect, next group!" Never wait more than 60 seconds for a missing person. Move on and come back.

Bridal Party Recommendations

Bridal party photos are your chance to show personality and have fun.

Standard Groupings

□ All bridesmaids together
□ All groomsmen together
□ Full wedding party together
□ Bride with each bridesmaid individually
□ Groom with each groomsman individually
□ Couple with full wedding party

Poses That Work for Groups

Bridesmaids

Walking toward camera together. Laughing candid — tell them to mess up the bride's hair on three. Serious fashion editorial — arms linked, looking away. Individual portraits of each.

Groomsmen

Walking toward camera together. Jumping shot — always gets laughs. Serious looking away together. Candid laughing together.

Full Wedding Party

Large group on steps or tiered location if available. Walking toward camera together — groom and bride in center. Fun shot — everyone pointing at the couple. Serious editorial wide shot.

Tips for Bridal Party Photos

Keep It Moving

Energy drops after 20 minutes of group shots. Get the serious shots first then do the fun ones. Couples remember the fun shots but they use the serious ones for albums and prints.

Hand and Arm Placement

Keep bridesmaids bouquets at waist height — not swinging at sides. Keep groomsmen hands in pockets or hands clasped in front — never dangling at sides.
