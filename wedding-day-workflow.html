Wedding Day Workflow

Before the Wedding Day

The night before:

Charge all 8 Canon LP-E6 batteries. Run both chargers simultaneously.

Format all SD and CF cards in-camera.

Pack your camera bag with both bodies, all three lenses, batteries, chargers, and cards.

Pack your lighting bag with 4 speedlights, 5 triggers, 4 light stands, 4 brackets, and AA batteries.

Check that everything is working. Test triggers. Test flash recycle times.

Day of arrival:

Arrive 30 minutes early. Scout the venue. Find good angles for each space.

Ask the coordinator or DJ for the timeline. Write it down.

Introduce yourself to key vendors — DJ, coordinator, venue staff.

Getting Ready (2-3 hours before ceremony)

Equipment check:

Start with fresh batteries in both cameras. Check battery icon in viewfinder.

Start with fresh cards in both cameras. Check shot count shows 0.

Shooting:

Use 50mm for details — dress, shoes, jewelry, rings.

Use 85mm for portraits of bride and groom separately.

Use 24mm for full room shots and group getting ready shots.

Mid-getting ready check:

Glance at battery icon. Should still be near 100%.

Check shot count. Should be under 500 images per camera.

Transition to ceremony:

Change batteries if under 80%. Better safe than sorry.

Check cards. Change if over 800 images per camera.

Ceremony (30-60 minutes)

Pre-ceremony:

Scout ceremony space. Find best angles for processional and recessional.

Test exposure. Lock settings before guests arrive.

During ceremony:

Use 50mm for most shots. 85mm for close-ups. 24mm for wide aisle shots.

Shoot RAW. Never JPEG.

Hold shutter during key moments — processional, vows, first kiss, recessional.

Post-ceremony check:

Check battery icon. Change if under 70%.

Check shot count. Change cards if over 900 images per camera.

This is your safest transition for card changes.

Cocktail Hour (45-60 minutes)

Lighting setup:

Setup reception lighting NOW if cocktail hour is in a different space than reception.

Place 4 light stands in corners or around perimeter.

Attach speedlights and triggers. Test all triggers fire.

Dial in power levels. Start at 1/16. Adjust based on room.

Shooting:

Use 50mm for candids and mingling guests.

Use 85mm for intimate portraits and details.

Use 24mm for wide room shots and group candids.

Mid-cocktail hour check:

Check battery icon. Change if under 60%.

Check shot count. Change cards if over 1,000 images per camera.

If cocktail hour is in reception space:

Setup lighting during ceremony instead.

Have stands ready to deploy as soon as ceremony ends.

Portraits (30-45 minutes)

Equipment check:

Change batteries before portraits if under 50%.

Change cards before portraits if over 1,000 images per camera.

Shooting:

Use 85mm for couple portraits. Creamy background separation.

Use 50mm for bridal party and family groups.

Use 24mm for large family formal shots.

Watch how the primary photographer directs couples. Learn their system.

Post-portraits check:

Check battery icon. Change if under 40%.

Check shot count. Change cards if over 1,100 images per camera.

This is your last safe transition before reception.

Reception Setup (15 minutes before guests enter)

If lighting not yet set:

Setup 4 light stands now. Place in corners or perimeter.

Attach speedlights and triggers. Test all triggers fire.

Dial in power levels. Start at 1/16. Test and adjust.

Camera settings lock:

Lens: 50mm

Aperture: f/2.5 to f/2.8

Shutter: 1/180 — never move this

ISO: start at 320-800

Flash settings:

Set all 4 speedlights to manual mode.

Start all at 1/16 power.

Test exposure. Adjust flash power first, then ISO.

Final equipment check:

Change batteries if under 30%. Reception is power-hungry.

Change cards if over 1,200 images per camera.

Have fresh batteries and cards ready in your pocket.

Reception (4-5 hours)

Grand entrance:

Use 50mm. Lock settings at f/2.5, 1/180, ISO 640.

Test exposure before guests enter.

First dance:

Hold shutter. Fire multiple frames. Get the best shot.

Toasts:

Shoot speaker and couple reactions simultaneously if possible.

Dancing:

Same settings all night. f/2.5, 1/180, ISO 640-800.

Adjust flash power if room gets darker. Bump to 1/8.

Adjust ISO only if flashes at full power and still too dark.

Hourly checks:

Every hour: check battery icon. Change if under 20%.

Every hour: check shot count. Change cards if over 1,300 images.

Change cards during slow moments — dinner service, between songs.

Never change cards during first dance or toasts.

End of night:

Final battery check. Change if under 15% for last 30 minutes.

Final card check. Change if over 1,400 images.

After the Wedding

Immediate backup:

Back up all cards to your computer the same night.

Never wait until morning. Cards can fail. Drives can fail.

Back up to two different drives if possible.

Equipment:

Charge all batteries overnight.

Format all cards in-camera after backup is verified.

Repack both bags. Everything ready for next wedding.

Editing:

Edit your best 20-30 images for portfolio.

Deliver full gallery to couple within 2 weeks.

Golden Rules

Check batteries before every major transition.

Check card space every hour. Change at 900-1,000 images.

Change cards during transitions only — never during key moments.

Setup lighting during cocktail hour — never when guests are in the room.

Back up everything the same night. Never wait until morning.
