Camera Settings for Wedding Photography

Shooting Manual

Manual mode means you control ISO, aperture, and shutter speed yourself. The camera does not adjust exposure automatically.

This is not manual focus. You still use autofocus.

The Exposure Triangle

Always adjust in this order:

STEP 1 — ISO FIRST

Push ISO up before touching anything else.

Canon sensors are clean up to ISO 1250 — fully usable with minimal grain. Start here and give yourself as much light as possible before adjusting shutter or aperture.

Bright outdoor ceremony: 100-400

Shaded outdoor: 400-800

Indoor good light: 800-1600

Dark reception: up to 3200-6400

ISO 1250 is your sweet spot before grain becomes noticeable.

STEP 2 — SHUTTER SPEED SECOND

Only adjust within focal length limits:

Still subjects: 2x focal length

50mm = minimum 1/100

85mm = minimum 1/160

24mm = minimum 1/50

Walking subjects: 2.5x focal length

50mm = minimum 1/125

85mm = minimum 1/200

24mm = minimum 1/60

Dancing without flash: 3x focal length

50mm = minimum 1/150

85mm = minimum 1/250

24mm = minimum 1/75

Do not go below these limits chasing more light. You will get blur every time.

STEP 3 — APERTURE LAST

Aperture is your last resort because it directly controls depth of field — how much of your image is in sharp focus front to back.

f/1.4 — extremely shallow depth of field. One eye sharp, other eye soft. Beautiful for individual portraits. Dangerous for groups.

f/2.8 — slightly more depth. Good for two people together.

f/4 — safe for small groups.

f/5.6 — most of a bridal party in focus. Use for formal group shots.

f/8 — everything sharp. Use for large family groups.

Opening aperture wider gets more light but less is in focus. Always be aware of that tradeoff before going wider.

The golden rule: Expose slightly dark rather than blown out. Dark recovers in editing. Blown out white dress does not. Ever.

White Balance

Shoot Auto White Balance. Doesn't matter much when shooting RAW. Adjust in post with zero quality loss.

Manual Kelvin if preferred:

Daylight: 5500K

Shade: 6500K

Tungsten: 3200K

Flash: 5500K

What is RAW

RAW is unprocessed sensor data. Full editing latitude in post. White balance, exposure, shadows, highlights — all fully adjustable without quality loss.

JPEG is compressed and baked in. Always shoot RAW. Never JPEG.

How to set RAW on 5D III and 5DS:

Menu → first camera icon → Image Quality → RAW

Set both card slots to RAW.

Canon Specific Settings

Picture Style: Neutral

Highlight tone priority: off

Long exposure noise reduction: off

High ISO noise reduction: off

Metering mode: Evaluative

AF mode: AI Servo for moving subjects, One Shot for stationary subjects

Drive mode: High speed continuous

Physical Controls

Main dial — shutter speed

Quick control dial — aperture

ISO button — hold and spin main dial to adjust

AF/Drive button — hold and spin to switch modes

Q button — quick control screen. Fastest way to adjust all settings on the fly

Back Button Focus

Separates focusing from shooting. Thumb on AF-ON focuses. Shutter button only shoots. Focus locks when you release AF-ON. Recompose and shoot cleanly.

How to set on 5D III and 5DS:

Menu → Custom Controls

Shutter button: metering only

AF-ON button: AF start

AF vs MF on the lens

Small switch on lens barrel.

AF = autofocus. Use this. Always.

MF = manual focus. Twist focus ring yourself.

Do not twist the range dial. The focus ring is the wider rubber ring on the lens barrel.

Shoot AF for weddings. Always.

FPS and Drive Modes

FPS = frames per second

5D Mark III = 6fps

5DS = 5fps

Hold shutter during key moments — first kiss, aisle walk, first dance. Camera fires multiple frames giving you the best shot.

Battery Management

How to change batteries:

Slide battery door latch on bottom of camera. Remove spent battery. Insert fresh battery with contacts facing inward. Close door until it clicks.

When to change:

Check battery icon in viewfinder before every major transition — getting ready to ceremony, ceremony to portraits, portraits to reception.

Never let a battery die mid-wedding. Change at 20% or earlier.

Start each wedding with 8 fully charged Canon LP-E6 batteries. You will use 4-6 per wedding.

Charging:

Charge all batteries the night before. Never rely on morning charging.

Have 2 chargers running simultaneously.

SD and CF Card Management

How to change cards:

SD card slot: door on right side of camera body. Push card in to eject.

CF card slot: door on right side below SD slot. Eject button releases card.

Best times to change cards:

Change cards during transitions only — never during key moments.

Safest times: cocktail hour, between ceremony and portraits, during dinner service.

Card limits:

128GB cards hold approximately 1,200-1,500 RAW images depending on camera.

Check shot count in viewfinder. Change cards at 900-1,000 images to stay safe.

Dual card slot backup:

5D III and 5DS have dual slots. Set both to RAW for automatic backup.

If one card fails, you have identical images on the second card.

Card brands:

SanDisk or Lexar only. Never off brand on cards.

Card failure is not worth saving $20.
