Building Your Portfolio and Getting Your First Booking

Building Your Portfolio Before You Have Paying Clients

You need a portfolio before you can book paying clients. Here is how to build one from scratch.

Third Shooting Images

The three weddings you third shot are your starting point. Edit your 20 best images from each wedding. Ask each photographer for written permission to use images in your portfolio. Most will say yes.

Free Shoots for Friends and Family

Find engaged couples in your personal network. Offer a free engagement session in exchange for portfolio use. Treat it exactly like a paid shoot. Golden hour in the desert produces stunning portfolio images.

Style Shoots

Rent or borrow a wedding dress. Find a couple willing to model. Book a venue you want to work at. Invite a florist who also needs portfolio work. Split the cost or do it free for everyone in exchange for portfolio usage rights.

What to Include

20 to 30 images maximum. Curated tight. Not everything. Variety — ceremony, portraits, details, reception. Your best light — golden hour. Real emotion — the laugh, the tear, the quiet moment. No watermarks. Mobile optimized.

Do You Need a Website?

Yes. Eventually. But not before your first booking. Your first client will come from someone who already knows you. They don't need a website. They need to see your photos and trust you.

Build After First Booking

Build your website after your first booking. Use the same stack proven across multiple real sites: GitHub for version control. Netlify for hosting — free tier. Custom domain from GoDaddy — around $12 per year. Build it yourself using HTML and CSS — no monthly Squarespace or Wix fees eating into profit.

What Your Website Needs

Homepage with hero image and clear call to action. Portfolio gallery — 20 to 30 best images. Pricing page — be transparent. About page — your story, your style, your approach. Contact form — Formspree, free. Blog — write about venues, tips, real weddings. SEO from day one — venue specific keywords, city specific keywords, style keywords.

The Knot, WeddingWire, and Zola Profiles

These three directories are the most important backlinks you can build. Free profiles on all three. High domain authority. Couples searching for photographers find you here.

Sign Up

The Knot: vendors.theknot.com/sign-up
WeddingWire: weddingwire.com/wedding-pro
Zola: zola.com/wedding-vendors/join

What to Include

20 of your best portfolio images. Consistent bio across all three. Pricing range. Your website URL. Phone number. Service area.

Keep Consistent

Keep everything consistent across all platforms. Same bio. Same pricing language. Same brand voice.

Instagram

Instagram is the most important social platform for wedding photographers. Couples find photographers on Instagram before they Google them.

Set Up

Business account. Username: your brand name. Bio: Scottsdale wedding photographer. Bright airy documentary style. Packages from $X. Link to your website.

What to Post

Real wedding images. Engagement session images. Behind the scenes. Venue features. Tips for couples.

Consistency

Consistency matters more than frequency. Three posts per week beats seven posts one week and nothing the next.

Hashtags That Work

#scottsdaleweddingphotographer #arizonaweddingphotographer #phoenixweddingphotographer #scottsdalewedding #arizonawedding #weddingphotography #documentaryweddingphotography

Tag venues in every post. Venues often repost — free exposure to their audience.

Facebook Page

Less important than Instagram for wedding photography but still worth having.

Create Business Page

Same name as your brand. Link to your website.

Post Content

Post your Instagram content here too — same images, same captions. Facebook and Instagram are connected — post once, publish to both.

Keeping Everything Consistent

Your brand should look and feel the same everywhere. Website, Instagram, Facebook, The Knot, WeddingWire, Zola.

Consistent Elements

Logo and wordmark. Color palette. Font choices. Bio language. Pricing language. Portfolio selection — same 20 images everywhere to start.

Why It Matters

Inconsistency confuses couples and signals disorganization. Pick your brand voice and use it everywhere.

What to Offer

Three package tiers to start:

Essential — $1,250

6 hours coverage. 400 to 500 edited images. Online gallery. Print release.

Standard — $1,750

8 hours coverage. 600 to 700 edited images. Online gallery. Print release. Engagement session.

Premium — $2,350

10 hours full day coverage. 800 to 900 edited images. Online gallery. Print release. Engagement session. Second shooter.

Offer a discount for your first three weddings. $750 to $1,000 range. You need portfolio weddings more than you need full price.

Getting Your First Booking

Your first booking will come from someone who already knows you.

Step 1 — Tell Everyone

Text message every person in your phone who is engaged or recently married. Post on your personal Facebook and Instagram. Tell coworkers, family, neighbors. "I'm starting a wedding photography business. I'm offering my first three weddings at a heavily discounted rate in exchange for portfolio use and an honest review."

Step 2 — Follow Up

If someone expresses interest follow up within 24 hours. Send them your portfolio. Offer a free 20 minute video call to answer questions.

Step 3 — The Consultation

Listen more than you talk. Ask about their wedding. Ask what matters most to them. Show images similar to their venue or style. Be honest about your experience. Most couples care more about personality fit than experience.

Step 4 — Send the Proposal

Follow up within 24 hours with a written proposal. Include package options, pricing, and availability. Keep it simple and clear.

The Contract

Never shoot a wedding without a signed contract. Even friends. Even family. Especially friends and family.

What Your Contract Needs

Names and contact info of both parties. Wedding date, time, location. Package details and price. Payment schedule — 50% deposit to book, 50% due 30 days before wedding. Cancellation policy. What happens if you get sick or have an emergency. Image delivery timeline. How images can be used. Print release for couple. Your right to use images in your portfolio.

Free Contract Resources

The Law Tog: thelawtogsociety.com. Offers photography specific contract templates. Worth the investment before your first wedding.

Legal Review

Never use a contract you wrote yourself without legal review.
