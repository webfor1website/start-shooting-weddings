Editing Your Wedding Photos in Lightroom

Adobe Lightroom Classic is the industry standard for wedding photographers. This page walks you through every step — importing your images from your memory cards, editing them to a consistent style, and exporting a finished gallery for your clients.

Installing Lightroom

Adobe Lightroom Classic

$10 per month via Adobe Creative Cloud

Download at: adobe.com/products/photoshop-lightroom

Lightroom Classic vs Lightroom CC

Always use Lightroom Classic. It stores files locally on your computer. Lightroom CC stores in the cloud and has limited features. Classic is the industry standard.

Importing Images

Step 1 — Get a card reader

Never import via USB cable from camera. Slower and risks draining battery mid import. Use a dedicated card reader:

ProGrade Digital SD reader — around $30 on Amazon

Lexar CF card reader — around $40 on Amazon

Step 2 — Open Lightroom

Click Import in bottom left.

Step 3 — Select your source

Choose your memory card from the left panel.

Step 4 — Choose destination

Create a folder structure that makes sense:

Wedding Photos / 2026 / 2026-06-15 Smith Wedding / RAW Files

Step 5 — Make a backup on import

In Import dialog check "Make a Second Copy To". Select an external hard drive. This is your automatic backup. Never skip this step.

Step 6 — Click Import

All RAW files load into your Lightroom catalog.

The Basic Panel

This is where 90% of your editing happens. Adjust in this order:

White Balance first

Temp slider — left is cooler (blue), right is warmer (orange). Tint slider — left is green, right is magenta.

For golden hour images go slightly warmer. For shade images go slightly cooler.

Use the eyedropper tool — click on something that should be white or neutral grey and Lightroom sets white balance automatically.

Exposure

Overall brightness of the image. Adjust until image feels right overall.

Highlights

Pull down to recover blown out bright areas. White dresses and white shirts — pull highlights down first.

Shadows

Push up slightly to open up dark areas. Brings out detail in darker parts of image.

Whites

Sets the brightest point. Hold Alt while dragging — areas that turn white are clipping (blown out).

Blacks

Sets the darkest point. Pull down slightly for rich deep blacks. Hold Alt while dragging — areas that turn black are clipping (pure black).

Contrast

Adds punch to the image. Use sparingly — too much looks harsh.

Clarity

Adds midtone contrast. Use very sparingly on portraits — it emphasizes skin texture.

Vibrance

Boosts less saturated colors first. Safer than saturation for skin tones. Use for boosting outdoor colors.

Saturation

Boosts all colors equally. Use carefully — easy to oversaturate.

BATCH EDITING — SAVE HOURS ON EVERY WEDDING

The single biggest time saver in Lightroom.

When you shoot a sequence of images in the same location with the same lighting and same camera settings — ISO, aperture, shutter speed — those images need the same edit.

Step 1 — Sort your images by lighting environment

Ceremony in bright sun is one group. Indoor getting ready is another group. Reception with flash is another group. Edit one image from each group first.

Step 2 — Edit your hero image

Pick the best image from the group. Edit it to perfection. White balance, exposure, highlights, shadows, all adjustments done.

Step 3 — Select all images in that group

Click the first image. Hold Shift and click the last. All images in the group are now selected.

Step 4 — Sync settings

Click Sync Settings button in bottom right of Develop module. A dialog box appears. Check the settings you want to copy across:

White Balance — yes
Exposure — yes
Highlights — yes
Shadows — yes
Whites — yes
Blacks — yes
Tone Curve — yes
HSL — yes
Lens Corrections — yes
Chromatic Aberration — yes

Click Synchronize.

All selected images now have identical edits applied.

Step 5 — Fine tune outliers

Some images in the group will need small individual adjustments. Scroll through and fix any that need tweaking. This takes minutes not hours.

Real world time savings

Without batch editing: Editing 800 images one by one — 8 to 12 hours.

With batch editing: Edit one image per lighting group. Sync to entire group. Fine tune outliers. Total time: 2 to 4 hours.

Typical wedding lighting groups

A typical wedding has 4 to 6 distinct lighting groups:

Getting ready — window light
Ceremony — outdoor bright sun
Family formals — open shade
Couple portraits — golden hour
Cocktail hour — mixed light
Reception — off camera flash

The workflow

Edit one image per group. Sync to the group. Fine tune. Export. Done.

This is how professional photographers deliver galleries in 2 weeks not 2 months.

Tone Curve

The tone curve gives you more precise control over contrast and color.

Basic S curve for contrast

Click on the curve in the shadows area and pull slightly down. Click in the highlights area and pull slightly up. Creates an S shape. Adds depth and contrast.

Lifting blacks for film look

Click the very bottom left point of the curve. Drag it slightly upward. Lifts the blacks. Creates a faded film aesthetic popular in wedding photography.

HSL Panel

HSL stands for Hue Saturation Luminance. Lets you adjust individual colors independently.

Most useful adjustments for wedding photography

Orange luminance — drag up to brighten skin tones. Makes skin look healthy and glowing.

Green saturation — drag up for lush outdoor greens.

Aqua and blue saturation — adjust skies and water.

Red saturation — pull down slightly to reduce red in skin.

Lens Corrections

Enable Profile Corrections

Click Lens Corrections panel. Check Enable Profile Corrections. Lightroom automatically detects your lens and removes distortion.

Remove Chromatic Aberration

Check Remove Chromatic Aberration. This removes the colored fringing you sometimes see on edges — especially in backlit images. One click. Done.

What is Chromatic Aberration

The problem

Purple or green fringing on the edges of subjects, especially in backlit images. Caused by different wavelengths of light bending differently through the lens.

The fix

Lens Corrections panel → check Remove Chromatic Aberration. Also in Manual tab — use the eyedropper to click directly on the fringing for more precise removal.

Applying Edits to Multiple Images

The sync workflow

Edit one image to your preferred style. Press Ctrl+A to select all images. Click Sync Settings in bottom right. Choose which settings to sync. Apply to all images at once.

Efficiency

This is how you edit a full wedding gallery efficiently.

Presets

A preset is a saved set of Lightroom adjustments you can apply with one click.

How to install presets

Download preset file (.xmp or .lrtemplate). In Lightroom: Develop module → Presets panel → right click → Import Presets. Select your downloaded file.

How to apply

Click any preset in the left panel to preview. Click to apply. Then fine tune as needed.

Good free preset packs to start

VSCO Film presets — classic film looks

RNI Films — realistic film simulation

Mastin Labs — industry favorite for weddings

Exporting

After editing you need to export two versions:

Full resolution for client delivery

File → Export
Image Format: JPEG
Quality: 90 to 100
Color Space: sRGB
Image Sizing: Don't Resize
Output Sharpening: Screen, Standard
File naming: [wedding date] [couple names] 001

Web optimized for portfolio

Same settings except:
Resize to fit: Long edge 2000 pixels
Quality: 80
File size will be much smaller. Loads faster on website.

Export entire gallery at once

Select all images. File → Export. Exports all selected images to your chosen folder.

Folder Structure and Backup

Organize every wedding the same way

Wedding Photos / [Year] / [Date] [Couple Names] / RAW / Edited / Exported for Client / Exported for Web

Back up immediately after every wedding

Primary copy on main hard drive
Second copy on external hard drive
Third copy on cloud storage — Google Drive or Backblaze

Never deliver a gallery without having at least two backup copies

Drives fail. Cards corrupt. Losing a wedding gallery is career ending.
